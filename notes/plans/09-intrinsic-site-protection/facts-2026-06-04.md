## Metric angles
1. **Representation geometry**: effective rank, repr-entropy, anisotropy, intrinsic-dim (TwoNN), adjacent-layer CKA, activation kurtosis/sparsity, residual-norm, update-norm, trajectory curvature.
2. **Information theory** (logit-lens + **tuned-lens-lite**): prediction entropy, KL-to-final, top-1 prediction depth.
3. **Parameter spectra** (per weight matrix, SVD): stable rank, effective rank, spectral norm, **HT-SR power-law α**, MLP gain.
4. **Attention structure** (per head): sink, retrieval, induction, attn-entropy/distance, KV/V norm, per-head spectral α.
5. **Forgetting coupling**: a-priori Fisher, gradient magnitude, **gradient-noise scale**; post-SFT weight drift & activation drift.

## The Janus-relevant fact (forgetting coupling, cross-family)
- **`dW_drift ↔ retrieval` (per head, mean-pooled) = +0.52** across all 4 families → **retrieval heads are where SFT weight-drift concentrates**, replicated on Qwen3.5 / GLM-4 / Qwen3 / Qwen2.5. This is the per-head, cross-family version of the H2 coupling: the read-side long-context site is the write-side drift site.
- Caveat: the earlier *single-model* "Fisher ↔ logit-lens-KL = +0.92" did **not** replicate as a clean cross-family per-layer fact (hd_fisher's only strong consistent partner is hd_grad_mag, a tautology). So the **drift↔retrieval** link is the robust forgetting fact; the Fisher↔info-layer link is model-specific.

## The "interesting" headline
A **single representation-collapse axis** runs through every family and ties three angles together:
**late layers** → residual norm ↑, prediction crystallizes (KL-to-final ↓, top-1 depth ↑), representations collapse (eff-rank ↓, anisotropy ↑), **massive activations emerge** (kurtosis ↑, sparsity ↑), and the **MLP down-proj spectrum** (stable rank) co-moves. Information formation, representation geometry, and parameter spectra are **the same phenomenon viewed three ways** — and it is vendor-independent (holds on GLM-4 as on Qwen).

## Caveats
- **Qwen3.5 hybrid**: only 8/32 layers are full-attention, so its per-head attention metrics (sink/retrieval/…) cover those 8 layers only (correctly remapped); per-layer representation/info/param facts use all 32 and are fully comparable.
- `fla`/`causal-conv1d` kernels not installed → Qwen3.5 uses the slower torch path (results unaffected).
- 4–8 generic text probes per model; per-layer ρ over 28–64 layers. Directional, not yet seed-banded.

# Exploration journey & corrected facts (2026-06-04 evening → ongoing)

> Honest log of how the facts evolved under scrutiny. Kept live as we probe boundaries.

## Stage 1 — the apparent coupling (later found confounded)
The 41-cell grid showed a **uniformly positive** per-head LC×CF coupling (every long-context metric ~ every forgetting metric, +0.17…0.56). Headline at the time: "the long-context heads are the forgetting heads." See `grid-metrics-2026-06-04.md`.

## Stage 2 — the reckoning (controls kill the head-level claim)
Adding the controls a reviewer would demand (partial-correlation out activation magnitude; **within-layer** to remove depth; per-model not pooled; bootstrap CIs; drop the qwen3.5 hybrid artifact) **dissolved the head-level coupling**. Per model it is weak/≈0/negative; the "survivor" `attn_distance~dW_drift` had within-layer ρ≈0 (a depth effect). The a-priori gradient signal is **null-to-negative**. → `mechanism-results-2026-06-04.md`. Lesson: cross-cell pooling + activation magnitude + depth + a hybrid model manufactured a phantom coupling.

## Stage 3 — the surviving phenomena (real, controlled)
Across the full-attention ladder (0.6→14B) + GLM-4 + Qwen2.5, with a-priori GSM8K gradients:
- **F-shield. Long-context structural heads are gradient-SHIELDED.** Within-layer ρ(grad, `attn_distance`) = −0.29…−0.60 and ρ(grad, `sink`) ≈ −0.05…−0.57 in **every** family → SFT gradients *avoid* long-reach/sink heads. Mediator: gradient ∝ activation magnitude (ρ(grad,`out_norm`) +0.35…+0.64), and long-context heads are lower-activation; on Qwen3, also low attention-entropy (saturated attention) → smaller gradient.
- **F-scale. The shielding fades with scale.** Layer-level ρ(long-ctx, grad): 0.6–1.7B ≈ −0.75 → 8B ≈ −0.14 → 14B ≈ 0. Small models shield hard; large models neutralize.
- **F-consequence.** This explains why **NIAH stays robust under SFT** (every H3 attempt): long-context isn't where forgetting lands.
- **F-collapse (from Stage-0, still holds).** The representation-collapse axis (eff-rank↓/anisotropy↑/massive-acts↑/prediction-crystallizes) is robust and vendor-independent — but it is *structural*, not the LC↔CF bridge we thought.

## Stage 4 — boundary probes (in progress)
Questions we are now testing on the surviving facts:
1. **Domain boundary (running).** Is the shielding domain-independent, or does training on a **long-context domain** (hotpotqa/narrativeqa) finally engage the long-context heads (gradient lands on them → real long-ctx forgetting risk)? a-priori grad recomputed per domain {gsm8k, wikitext, triviaqa, hotpotqa, narrativeqa} on Qwen3-8B + GLM-4-9B.
2. **Scale boundary.** Does the shielding only fade, or **reverse** (long-ctx heads become *vulnerable*) at 30B+? Run on GLM-4-32B / Qwen3-30B-A3B (H200).
3. **Carrier disentangle.** Of {attn_distance, sink, low-entropy, low out_norm}, which is the *causal* shield property? (partial-out each in turn.)
4. **A-priori → actual.** Confirm the a-priori shielding produces **actual low post-hoc drift** under a valid LoRA SFT (closes the loop).
5. **Depth profile.** Where in depth does the shield live, and how does that move with scale?

### Boundary result B2 — scale (2026-06-04 ~19:20): shielding fades then REVERSES by 32B
Within-layer ρ(a-priori grad, `attn_distance`) vs model size (GSM8K gradients):
0.6B −0.50 · 1.7B −0.58 · 4B −0.60 · 8B −0.57 · 14B −0.55 · GLM-4-9B −0.29 · **GLM-4-32B +0.11**.
And at 32B the activation driver flips too (`out_norm` within +0.61 @8B → **−0.07 @32B**), `sink` +0.13.
**F-scale (refined).** The shielding is strong/robust through ~14B, weakens in the GLM family, and by **32B is gone and mildly reverses** (long-context heads become *slightly* gradient-engaged) — and gradient is **no longer activation-driven** at 32B. → the original "long-context heads are vulnerable" intuition may have a kernel of truth **only at large scale**; worth checking a 30B-MoE next. (n=1 at 32B; weak magnitude — don't over-claim.)

### Boundary result B1 — domain (2026-06-04 ~19:15): shielding is a TASK-objective effect
Recomputed the a-priori shielding (within-layer ρ grad vs `attn_distance`) using gradients from different SFT domains, on Qwen3-8B / GLM-4-9B:

| domain | qwen3-8b | glm4-9b |
|---|---|---|
| gsm8k (math task) | −0.57 | −0.29 |
| triviaqa (QA) | −0.48 | −0.43 |
| **hotpotqa (long-ctx multi-hop)** | **−0.57** | **−0.43** |
| **narrativeqa (long-doc)** | **−0.49** | **−0.39** |
| **wikitext (plain LM)** | **+0.09** | **−0.04** |

**F-domain.** The shielding (i) **holds even for long-context *tasks*** (hotpot/narrativeqa stay ≈ −0.5) — so it's NOT about the task's context length; and (ii) **vanishes under the plain language-modeling objective** (wikitext ≈ 0). → the gradient-shielding of long-context heads is a property of **task/instruction (Q→A) fine-tuning**: short-answer losses concentrate gradient on local heads, sparing long-reach/sink heads. `out_norm`-as-driver mirrors this (+0.5…+0.6 for tasks, ≈0 for wikitext). **Practical:** instruction-SFT shields long-context (explains NIAH robustness); continued LM-pretraining would *not* → that's where long-context forgetting could actually occur. (My pre-test hypothesis — "long-ctx-domain SFT engages long-ctx heads" — is refuted; the carrier is the *objective*, not the domain.)
