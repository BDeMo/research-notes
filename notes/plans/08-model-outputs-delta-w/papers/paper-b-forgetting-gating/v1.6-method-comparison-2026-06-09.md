# v1.6: how Full / LoRA / MoE / lightweight-compressor differ (training + properties)

> Comparison of the four ways to add knowledge/adaptation, by TRAINING METHOD and PROPERTIES.
> The axis that matters for v1.6: where the new knowledge lives, and whether it is detachable.
> House rule: no em-dashes. 2026-06-09.

## The one-line distinction
They differ in **where the new knowledge is put** and **whether it can be turned off**:
Full (into all weights, cannot detach) -> LoRA (into an added branch, can disable) -> MoE (into an added
expert gated by a router, conditional) -> lightweight compressor (into EXTERNAL latents, fully detachable).
Moving along that spectrum, cross-domain preservation (anti-forgetting) improves and the do-no-harm floor
gets cleaner.

## Comparison table

## Per-method, the distinctive characteristic
- **Full fine-tune.** Trains everything on the new data. Most capacity to learn, but the new knowledge is
  entangled into all weights, so it overwrites old capabilities (catastrophic forgetting) and cannot be
  detached. No do-no-harm floor.
- **LoRA.** Freezes the base, trains a low-rank branch `W + BA` on chosen matrices. New knowledge is in a
  localized, KNOWN branch, but the branch is added on EVERY forward, so it shifts behavior on held-out
  inputs too (real forgetting, milder than full). You can disable it, so a floor exists if you choose to use it.
- **MoE (incremental expert).** Adds a new expert FFN for the new knowledge and trains the router to send the
  right tokens to it (existing experts frozen, load-balancing aux loss on the router). Use of the new
  knowledge is CONDITIONAL on the router, which both limits forgetting (old tokens keep old experts) and
  gives a built-in gate; forgetting can still leak when the router re-routes old tokens.
- **Lightweight compressor (wrapper).** Freezes the base entirely and trains a small external module that
  turns a long context into K latent tokens the frozen base reads. Crucially the task knowledge is NOT
  baked into the model: it remains in the (compressed) context, and the module only learns HOW to compress.
  The latents are injected at inference and are fully detachable, so the worst case is the exact base
  (do-no-harm floor by construction) and held-out forgetting is exactly zero.

## Why this ordering motivates the in-domain vs cross-domain figure
As you go Full -> LoRA -> MoE -> compressor, the new knowledge becomes less baked-in and more detachable,
so **cross-domain score is increasingly preserved** (anti-forgetting). On a plot of in-domain (x) vs
cross-domain (y) average score: weight-editing methods (Full, LoRA, and MoE to a lesser degree) sit BELOW
the y=x diagonal (in-domain up, cross-domain dropped = forgetting), while the lightweight compressor stays
near the diagonal / high on y (cross-domain preserved). That contrast is the figure's message: the
compressor generalizes and does not forget, precisely because the base is untouched and the module is detachable.
