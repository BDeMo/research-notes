## Shared definitions (so every experiment is comparable)
- **"compression broke" label** (per input, the harness target): both `broke_vs_full` and `broke_vs_base`
  per Decision 3 (eps default 0, swept).
- **read budget t = how much the model is allowed to GENERATE before we read a signal and decide
  "keep the compressed context vs interrupt and re-read the full original text".** Cheapest to costliest:
  - **prefill (t=0)**: ingest [compressed latents + question], compute hidden states + the first-token
    distribution, emit NO token yet. Read = first-token confidence/entropy, hidden geometry, divergence
    from the uncompressed case. Cost = one forward over a short (compressed) input.
  - **first-1 / first-2 / first-4 / first-8 (t=k)**: emit k tokens, read the signal accumulated over them.
  - **full (t=full)**: generate the whole answer (~16 tokens), read from the full output.
  - cost order: prefill < first-1 < ... < full < best-of-N (= full x N). Bet: prefill/first-1 approx full,
    far cheaper than full or best-of-N.
- **policies compared everywhere**: `always-compress` (cheap, risky) | `always-full` (safe, costly) |
  `best-of-N` x {self-consistency, first-token-conf, perplexity} (cost N x) | **`circuit-breaker` (ours:
  read the signal at budget t, keep compressed else interrupt->full)** | `oracle` (knows broke).
- **metrics**: task accuracy; token/compute cost (amortised: compressed prefill + read of t tokens +
  interrupt-rate x full re-read); interrupt rate; **interrupt earliness** (the t we decide at); detection
  AUROC of the signal vs broke (both definitions).
- **models**: q8, glm, q14, q25, mistral (+ phi where it runs) + **OLMoE-1B-7B (real MoE, E2b)**.
  **compressors**: Gist-lite, Cartridge-lite, mem-X (ours). **benches**: trivia, squad, hotpot,
  narrativeqa, ms_marco, quality, musr (+ ruler noted).

## What the interrupt signal IS (signal design) -- needed to choose Decision 4
Core idea: if compression dropped critical info, the model's internal state / output behavior after
reading the compressed context shows tells. The "signal" = those tells. Two scenarios:

**Scenario (2), location UNKNOWN (the general read; all computable AT PREFILL, no generation needed):**
- `conf_w` first-token top-1 probability (low = unsure); `margin_w` top1-top2 gap; `entropy_w` spread of
  the first-token distribution.
- `kl_w0` KL(first-token dist WITH compressed ctx || WITHOUT ctx): how much the compressed context moved
  the model's immediate belief. Near 0 = the compression carried ~no usable info (likely broke).
- `first_tok_agree` does the top-1 token with vs without compression match.
- hidden geometry after reading the compressed ctx: effective-rank ratio, top-5 mass, drift (representation
  collapse / anomaly).
- (recursion to scenario 1) the compressor's OWN internals: slot norms, `delta_last` (how much the memory
  perturbs the output) = did the compressor actually inject information.

**Scenario (1), location KNOWN (read the knowledge module):**
- LoRA: branch activation norm + branch-vs-base output divergence (did the branch that holds the needed
  knowledge fire for this input).
- MoE: router top-k choice, routing weights/entropy, new-expert weight (was the right expert activated).

**Why generate tokens at all (why Decision 4 is not just "prefill"):** prefill only reveals the model's
belief about the FIRST token. Breakage often surfaces LATER in the answer (confident start, then hedging
"the document does not say", self-contradiction, drift). Generating k tokens adds: confidence trajectory,
hedging detection, divergence of the partial answer from the full-context partial answer. More tokens =
potentially more breakage signal, but more cost. **Whether prefill already suffices vs needs a few tokens
is exactly the EMPIRICAL question E1 measures.** Prior (from the v1.5 signal study): cheap base-uncertainty
at prefill is about as good as the rich menu, so prefill/first-token likely suffices, but we verify.

## E0 [CENTRAL RESULT] The harness circuit-breaker vs the alternatives
- **Goal**: show a single-pass, early-signal circuit breaker matches `always-full` accuracy at a fraction
  of the cost, and beats `always-compress` and `best-of-N` on the accuracy-cost frontier.
- **Setup**: per (compressor, model, bench, item): compute `native_mem`, `native_full`, the early signal,
  cost. Route by the early signal. 5-fold CV for the signal threshold.
- **Baselines**: always-compress, always-full, best-of-N (N in {2,4,8}), oracle.
- **New vs v1.5**: adds the **best-of-N** baseline and the **cost+earliness** axes to the existing
  two-track gate (`gate3_route` Track B). Mostly reuses `gx_/cmb_` cells + a best-of-N logger.
- **Deliverable**: the accuracy-vs-cost frontier figure + "match full at X% cost, beat best-of-N at 1/N cost."

## E1 [CORE NOVELTY] How EARLY can we detect a broken compression?
- **Goal**: prove detection works from INTERMEDIATE states (small read budget t), not only after full
  output or best-of-N. This is the v1.6 thesis.
- **Setup**: log the interrupt signal at t in {prefill, first-token, first-2, first-4, first-8, full}.
  Plot **detection AUROC(t)** vs the `broke` label, per compressor/model. Compare to best-of-N's
  selection quality (which needs full decode x N).
- **Hypothesis**: AUROC(prefill/first-token) approx AUROC(full); a few tokens give most of the signal.
- **New vs v1.5**: v1.5 logged first-token only; E1 adds the **multi-t curve** (needs decode-step logging)
  and the explicit "early approx full, and cheaper than best-of-N" claim.
- **New code**: extend `base_signals.collect` / the loggers to record signals at several decode steps.

## E2 [SCENARIO 1] Known knowledge location -> read the interrupt from the branch/router
- **E2a LoRA branch signal.** Knowledge sits in a LoRA branch on a frozen base. Read the BRANCH behavior
  (branch activation norm, branch-vs-base output divergence, per-layer) as the interrupt signal; test
  whether it flags `broke` for THIS input.
  - **New vs v1.5**: `mix_sft --signals` already logs adapted-vs-base `base_signals` (that IS the branch
    divergence). E2a re-targets it to the `broke` label + reports branch-signal AUROC, and adds explicit
    per-branch (per-LoRA-module) readouts.
  - **Reuse**: `fgt_*` cells (landed). **New code**: log per-branch activation norms in `mix_sft`.
- **E2b MoE expert+router signal.** Incrementally add/train an expert for new knowledge; read the ROUTER
  (top-k choice, routing entropy, new-expert weight) as the interrupt signal.
  - **Gap**: no real MoE base local. Two options: (i) **emulate** with our `moe_route` null+experts (done,
    but it is a routing emulation, not a real MoE router); (ii) **fetch an MoE base** (OLMoE-1B-7B or
    Qwen3-30B-A3B) and run the floor+gate+router-signal pipeline. Recommend (ii) for a real router signal,
    time permitting; (i) as the fallback narrative.
- **Output**: "when knowledge location is known, the branch/router behavior is a cheap, mechanistic
  interrupt signal" + its AUROC vs the location-agnostic signal.

## E3 [SCENARIO 2] Unknown location -> frozen-base lightweight compressor + relevance + recursion
- **E3a Compressor library.** Train lightweight compressors on multiple datasets; cross-apply (train D,
  eval D'); map when compression breaks by distribution distance.
  - **Reuse**: the transfer grid `xf_*` (running) IS the compressor lib x eval grid. Analysis via
    `grid_transfer.py`. **Deliverable**: a break-rate heatmap (compressor-train x eval-bench).
- **E3b Prompt-context relevance probe.** Train a lightweight head on the compressed representation (+ the
  query) to predict prompt-relevance / `broke`, without knowing knowledge location.
  - **New code**: a small relevance head on top of the compressor latents + first-token base response;
    trained on the compressor-lib data; evaluated as a `broke` detector.
- **E3c Recursion to (1).** Treat the compressor as the "known module": read its OWN internals
  (slot norms, `delta_last`, compressed-vs-base divergence `comp_unc`) as the interrupt signal.
  - **Reuse**: `sig_*` + `comp_unc` (logged). Re-target to `broke` + early budget.
- **Output**: "without knowing location, a lightweight relevance read + the compressor's own internals
  give the interrupt signal; the compressor lib shows the break pattern."

## E4 [CROSS-CUTTING] Which intermediate signal best flags `broke`, at the EARLY budget?
- **Goal**: the unified signal study, re-targeted from v1.5's "useful" to v1.6's harness label `broke`,

## E5 [DEPLOYMENT] Cost / latency accounting (the payoff)
- **Goal**: the deployment number. Cost(circuit-breaker) = compressed prefill + early read (t tokens) +

- **Reuse**: `mem_tokens`/`full_tokens` (logged) + the interrupt earliness from E1. Analysis only.
- **Deliverable**: "match full at X% tokens / Y% latency; best-of-N pays N x for no oracle gain."
