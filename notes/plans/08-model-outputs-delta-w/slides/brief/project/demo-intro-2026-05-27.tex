\begin{frame}{Motivation: Why RCA Foundation Model}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item RCA turns noisy operational evidence into root cause, supporting
      evidence, confidence, severity, and recommended fix.
    \item For Nokia, this matters in three places:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item internal development: faster debugging and less manual triage;
        \item industrial LLM deployment: models must operate over tools, traces,
          logs, tickets, and service topology;
        \item telecom operations: failures are complex, multi-service, and
          evidence-heavy.
      \end{itemize}
    \item Goal: make complex RCA workflows easier for humans to operate and
      easier for models to assist.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Problem: Do We Already Have an RCA LLM?}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Most strong public LLMs are optimized for broad consumer and office use:
      chat, writing, coding, math, and general reasoning.
    \item RCA is a specialized industrial domain:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item evidence is long, noisy, and multi-source;
        \item outputs must be structured and actionable;
        \item correctness depends on service-level grounding, not fluent text.
      \end{itemize}
    \item Since the largest AI companies cover the largest markets first, RCA
      capability is not usually a first-class target.
    \item Therefore the practical question is: can we train an RCA foundation
      model from open-source bases?
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Why Data Is the Center of the Project}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item If no ready-to-use RCA foundation model exists, the core asset becomes
      RCA data.
    \item High-quality data sampling controls domain generalization:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item broad cases teach coverage across services and fault types;
        \item precise labels teach what the correct root cause should be;
        \item evidence labels teach the model not to hallucinate;
        \item preference / reward data teaches which answer is better.
      \end{itemize}
    \item Domain data is also the largest bottleneck: RCA gold answers are
      expensive, expert-heavy, and often private.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Post-Training Pipeline: Data Needed at Each Stage}
  \scriptsize
  \begin{table}
    \begin{tabular}{p{0.18\textwidth}p{0.32\textwidth}p{0.38\textwidth}}
      \toprule
      Stage & Data needed & What it teaches \\
      \midrule
      SFT & Human-labeled gold RCA answers & Output schema, RCA vocabulary, service/root-cause mapping. \\
      RL / PPO & Reward model or task evaluator for RCA outputs & Prefer grounded, correct, actionable RCA answers over fluent but shallow ones. \\
      On-policy distillation & Current model samples plus evaluator-selected better answers & Adjust the model's own generation distribution and reduce exposure bias. \\
      Preference / DPO-style training & Pairwise better/worse RCA answers & Learn quality ranking without only copying one gold answer. \\
      Tool / agent training & Logs, traces, tool calls, and success criteria & Learn when to inspect evidence before final RCA prediction. \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Simple Example: SFT vs On-Policy Training}
  \begin{block}{Evidence}
    Service A is slow. Service B has timeout errors. Database C shows
    connection failures.
  \end{block}
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
      \begin{block}{SFT target}
        Root cause: Database C connection failure.\\
        Evidence: B timeout plus DB connection error.\\
        Fix: inspect DB network and connection pool.
      \end{block}
    \end{column}
    \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
      \begin{block}{On-policy correction}
        Model sample A: blame Service B.\\
        Model sample B: blame Database C.\\
        Evaluator rewards B, then RL/PPO or distillation increases B-style answers.
      \end{block}
    \end{column}
  \end{columns}
  \vspace{0.4em}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item SFT teaches the RCA language; on-policy training teaches the model to
      behave correctly under its own generation distribution.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Current Difficulty: General Capability Drop}
  \scriptsize
  \begin{table}
    \begin{tabular}{lrrrr}
      \toprule
      Checkpoint & IFEval & MMLU & GSM8K & TruthfulQA \\
      \midrule
      $q25_{base}$ & 0.680 & 0.754 & 0.795 & 0.647 \\
      $q25_{mix}$ & 0.655 & 0.751 & 0.715 & 0.589 \\
      Drop & -0.025 & -0.003 & -0.080 & -0.058 \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}
  \normalsize
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Main lesson: RCA SFT improves domain behavior, but direct SFT can move
      general capability.
    \item This motivates a full post-training pipeline and stronger regression
      checks, not only one SFT checkpoint.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Next Deliverable 3: Harness Iteration System}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item RCA foundation model quality depends on the model and the tools around
      it.
    \item When the base model updates, the harness must also track:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item tool-calling schemas;
        \item skill prompts and reusable RCA procedures;
        \item MCP / external tool interfaces;
        \item evaluation sets and reward models;
        \item regression reports across old and new checkpoints.
      \end{itemize}
    \item Long-term goal: continuously improve the RCA model and its operating
      system together.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Takeaway}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Motivation: Nokia needs RCA models for complex telecom and industrial
      operations.
    \item Evidence: public base models have weak RCA benchmark performance; SFT
      significantly improves RCA behavior on Qwen2.5.
    \item Boundary: SFT alone does not solve exposure bias, evaluator shortage,
      evidence grounding, or forgetting.
    \item Direction: build the full post-training pipeline and the GUI/harness
      system needed to keep RCA models improving over time.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
