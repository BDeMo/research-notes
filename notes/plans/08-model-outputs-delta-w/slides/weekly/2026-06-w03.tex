\begin{frame}{Continuity From v1.6 / v1.7.3}
  \begin{block}{Earlier question}
    Can a learned, gated soft-prompt memory around a frozen base usefully compress long context?
  \end{block}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item v1.6/v1.7 built the gated compressor and reported encouraging tool/RCA numbers.
    \item A code review then found a \textbf{train/eval leakage} (71--96\%); the clean redo is \textbf{v1.7.3}.
    \item v1.7.4 then \emph{tried to fix} long-context compression (length-adaptive memory, scaling); v1.7.5
      adds training-stability rigor.
    \item The story moves from ``build a better compressor'' to a \textbf{capacity-bound diagnosis} plus the
      \textbf{do-no-harm cure} it prescribes.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{This Milestone: Main Message}
  \begin{block}{Diagnosis $\to$ Cure}
    Soft/KV context compression for a frozen LLM is \textbf{capacity-bound}; the deployable contribution is the
    \textbf{do-no-harm gate} prescribed by that diagnosis, not a better compressor.
  \end{block}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Diagnosis}: never beats full; trivial truncation matches full on short context; no scaling;
      length-adaptive memory does not help; the memory is OOD.
    \item \textbf{Breadth}: holds across 9 compressors and 5 domains, under a leak-free / held-out / stable-training protocol.
    \item \textbf{Cure}: the same signals that explain \emph{why} compression fails reveal \emph{when} it fails
      $\Rightarrow$ a compressor-agnostic gate detects unsafe compression and falls back (never hurts).
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Diagnosis 1: Bounded by Full and by Truncation}
  \small
  \begin{table}\centering
    \begin{tabular}{lccc}
      \toprule
      bench & full & GCM (ours) & \textbf{trunc} (no train) \\
      \midrule
      bfcl\_live\_simple & 0.91 & 0.53 & \textbf{0.91} \\
      bfcl\_simple & 0.99 & 0.17 & \textbf{0.99} \\
      bfcl\_live\_multiple & 0.91 & \textbf{0.72} & 0.46 \\
      toolace & 0.95 & 0.14 & 0.01 \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item No compressor beats \textbf{full}; on short context trivial \textbf{truncation matches full} and beats the learned compressor.
    \item The trained encoder beats truncation on only one bench (ctx $\gg K$). Matched-budget: GCM $\ge$ Cartridge $\gg$ Gist.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Diagnosis 2: No Scaling, Length-Adaptive Does Not Help}
  \small
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \begin{column}{0.5\textwidth}
      \begin{block}{No scaling (mix of 12 datasets)}
        avg compress-acc flat at $\sim$0.26 across
        \begin{itemize}\scriptsize
          \item compute: $3.5\!\times\!10^{15}\to8.4\!\times\!10^{16}$ FLOPs ($24\times$)
          \item data: $8\times$; depth $N{=}4\to28$ ($N{=}4$ best)
        \end{itemize}
      \end{block}
    \end{column}
    \begin{column}{0.5\textwidth}
      \begin{block}{Length-adaptive $\approx$ fixed-K}
        \scriptsize $\Delta$(ch16a $-$ k64) within $\pm0.02$ on every shared bench, incl.\ long ctx (rca 0.094 vs 0.104).
      \end{block}
    \end{column}
  \end{columns}
  \vspace{0.3em}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item More data / depth / compute does \textbf{not} lift compression; chunking the memory buys nothing.
    \item \textbf{Diagnosis 3 (OOD)}: nearest-vocabulary cosine of memory $\approx0.10$ (decodes to gibberish) $\Rightarrow$ the read interface is the wall.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{The Cure: Compressor-Agnostic Do-No-Harm Gate}
  \small
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Keep the base \textbf{frozen} (non-merged adapter, off for fallback) $\Rightarrow$ full context always recoverable (never hurts by construction).
    \item Gate signal (base confidence + reconstruction) is read off \emph{any} compressor's memory $\Rightarrow$ \textbf{transfers across compressors}.
    \item On a \emph{different} compressor (Cartridge): failure-detection \textbf{AUROC 0.6--0.8}, held-out gated acc $\approx$ full.
    \item Honest: detection is near chance on most benches; there the value is conservative do-no-harm (gated acc $=$ full), with real detection on conversational benches.
    \item In progress: gate precision/recall/F1 on the embedding-wise baselines' tokens (the cross-compressor test).
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

  \small

    \emph{Context compression for frozen LLMs is capacity-bound}: a cross-compressor, cross-domain diagnosis,
    plus the do-no-harm gate it prescribes. We keep it diagnosis-led even where a method performs well.
  \end{block}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Rigor}: leak-free disjoint splits; held-out 5-fold gate; matched-budget \emph{faithful} baselines
      (ICAE/AOC/Beacon/500x re-impls); args-aware tool scoring.
    \item \textbf{v1.7.5 stability}: gradient accumulation + LR warmup/cosine + train/val tracking $\Rightarrow$ the
      negatives are not a training artifact (forecloses the ``you trained it wrong'' critique).
