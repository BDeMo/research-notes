\begin{frame}{Continuity From June Week 1}
  \begin{block}{Week 1 result}
    The wrapper learns useful compression only in a bounded regime; therefore place a
    do-no-harm \emph{gate} between wrapper memory and the frozen base.
  \end{block}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Week 2 stress-tested the gated compressor on \textbf{agentic tool-use + RCA}.
    \item We \textbf{hardened the evaluation} (leak-free, held-out, matched-budget) and re-ran clean (v1.7.3).
    \item The clean results moved the story from ``build a better compressor'' to
      ``\textbf{a compressor-agnostic robustness layer}.''
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{This Week: Main Message}
  \begin{block}{Reframed contribution}
    Not a better compressor --- a \textbf{robustness layer}: an external confidence /
    self-verification signal that detects when compression is unsafe and falls back to full
    context (do-no-harm). It works on top of \emph{any} compressor.
  \end{block}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Compression alone is \textbf{capacity-bound} and \textbf{never beats full} --- which is
      exactly why a detect-and-fallback layer is needed.
    \item The gate is \textbf{compressor-agnostic}: it recovers $\approx$ full on our method
      \emph{and} on a faithful Cartridge baseline.
    \item Evaluation hardened to leak-free / held-out / matched-budget; honest numbers throughout.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Why The Reframe: What The Clean Data Shows}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Fixed-size soft-prompt compression is \textbf{capacity-bound}:
      correlation(context length, value-add) $= -0.4$ --- it helps at low compression ratio
      (short context, $\le 3\times$) and \textbf{fails at high ratio} ($\ge 9\times$, long context).
    \item On every tool benchmark, compressed accuracy stays \textbf{below full context};
      the realized compute saving from compression alone is $\approx 0$.
    \item So the deployable value is not ``compress better'' but
      \textbf{``detect bad compression and fall back''} --- a safety layer usable with any compressor.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

    \node[box, above right=0.5cm and 0.8cm of gate] (cp) {Compress path\\$F_\theta(q,m)$};
    \node[box, below right=0.5cm and 0.8cm of gate] (fb) {Fallback:\\full context};
    \node[box, right=0.8cm of $(cp)!0.5!(fb)$] (out) {Answer $y$};

    \draw[arr] (ctx) -- (comp);

  \vspace{0.4em}
  \begin{block}{Core idea}
    Any compressor makes memory; a confidence / self-verification signal scores it; the gate
    compresses when safe and falls back to full when not --- never worse than full.
  \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Core Clean Findings (v1.7.3, leak-free, in-task)}
  \small
  \begin{table}
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{lccccc}
      \toprule
      Tool benchmark & no-ctx & full & GCM compress & gate gAcc & detect AUROC \\
      \midrule
      BFCL live-multiple & 0.01 & 0.91 & 0.72 & 0.89 & 0.75 \\
      BFCL live-simple   & 0.01 & 0.91 & 0.53 & 0.92 & 0.88 \\
      BFCL simple        & 0.02 & 0.99 & 0.17 & 0.99 & 0.52 \\
      ToolACE            & 0.01 & 0.95 & 0.14 & 0.94 & 0.53 \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}
  \vspace{-0.2em}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Compression stays \textbf{below full} everywhere; the \textbf{gate gAcc $\approx$ full}
      (held-out, within $-0.05 .. +0.03$).
    \item Detection works where compression is learnable (live benches AUROC 0.75--0.88);
      elsewhere the gate \textbf{safely abstains} (falls back to full).
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Headline: The Gate Is Compressor-Agnostic}
  \small
  \begin{table}
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{llccc}
      \toprule
      Compressor & benchmark & compress & detect AUROC & gate gAcc \\
      \midrule
      GCM (ours)         & BFCL live-simple   & 0.53 & 0.88 & 0.92 \\
      \textbf{Cartridge} & BFCL parallel      & 0.28 & \textbf{0.81} & 0.93 \\
      \textbf{Cartridge} & BFCL live-multiple & 0.51 & \textbf{0.73} & 0.90 \\
      \textbf{Cartridge} & BFCL live-simple   & 0.31 & 0.70 & 0.90 \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}
  \begin{block}{Why it matters}
    The same confidence gate, applied to a \emph{different} compressor (Cartridge), still
    detects its failures (AUROC 0.6--0.8) and recovers $\approx$ full. The robustness layer is
    not tied to our encoder --- it is a reusable safety net for any compressor.
  \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Evaluation Rigor Added This Week}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Leak-free splits}: a seed-independent train/eval partition (caught and fixed a
      train/eval overlap; all tool results re-run clean as v1.7.3).
    \item \textbf{Held-out gate}: 5-fold cross-validated threshold (removes in-sample optimism).
    \item \textbf{Matched-budget baselines}: Cartridge / Gist re-trained at equal data, steps, and memory.
    \item \textbf{Args-aware tool scoring} (function name + arguments) and \textbf{multi-seed} confidence intervals (in progress).
    \item \textbf{Breadth}: tool benchmarks from \textbf{5 independent teams}
      (BFCL, API-Bank, ToolACE, Hermes, Glaive) plus RCA.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Next Steps}
  \begin{enumerate}

    \item \textbf{Transfer matrix}: in-task / cross-task / cross-domain for the gated system + baselines.
    \item \textbf{Args-aware verdict}: do the tool wins survive when arguments must also be correct?
    \item \textbf{Agnostic claim}: apply the gate to a strong external compressor (LLMLingua).
    \item \textbf{Paper identity}: lock ``diagnosis + do-no-harm robustness layer'' (well supported by the clean data).
  \end{enumerate}
\end{frame}
