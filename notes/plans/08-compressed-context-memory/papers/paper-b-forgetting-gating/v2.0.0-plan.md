# Paper B — v2.0.0 plan: long-context **necessity** + **domain generalization** (not efficiency)

> Companion to [`paper-logic-v1.8.0.md`](results-v1.8.0/paper-logic-v1.8.0.md), [`results-v1.8.0/results-v1.8.0.md`](results-v1.8.0/results-v1.8.0.md), and the lit DB [`references-longcontext.md`](references-longcontext.md).
> **What changes vs v1.8.0:** the *story*. v1.8.0 sold compress+gate as a **cost/efficiency** play (do-no-harm gate ≥ full at lower cost). v2.0.0 sells **necessity + generalization**.

---

## 1. The new one-line thesis
> Some inputs **don't fit** — the evidence exceeds the window (or the KV/compute budget), so the model literally **cannot read it**. The question is not "compress to save cost" but **"how do you get the information in at all, and does that representation transfer across domains?"** We compress the *whole* context into a small soft memory M that (a) **gets evidence in that truncation/recent-window baselines lose**, and (b) **a do-no-harm gate trusts only when it's verified-safe (out-of-sample)** — so quality is never worse than the best feasible read. *Long-context is the necessity; domain generalization is the test; the gate is the safety contract.*

**Why this is honest given our own results (v1.8.0):**
- We **proved** (TXL/StreamingLLM baseline) that when a recent window suffices, raw windows beat compression → compression is **not** a general win.
- We **proved** (held-out CV gate) that "gated > full" does **not** survive when full is strong → the efficiency/accuracy-supremacy story is dead.
- What **survives**: on `quality` (evidence spread, over-window) full & TXL collapse to ~0.11–0.14 while compress = 0.39 (3×) — i.e., **the only defensible win is the necessity regime.** v2.0.0 builds the whole paper on that regime + on whether M transfers across domains.

## 2. Three claims (v2.0.0)
- **C1 — Necessity (headline):** in the over-window / evidence-spread regime, compressed memory **strictly dominates** the feasible no-compression baselines (truncated-full, StreamingLLM/TXL recent-window, even retrieval when evidence is diffuse). *Evidence: RULER length-sweep crossover + QuALITY/NarrativeQA/∞Bench/NoCha + CMG-RCA (27k) motivation.*
- **C2 — Domain generalization:** a compressor trained on domain set A keeps a useful fraction of its compress-quality on unseen domain B (in-task ≥ cross-task ≥ cross-domain, but cross-domain ≫ no-context), and we **characterize *intrinsically* why** (which feature/attention signatures predict transfer; §K probes). *This is the new scientific contribution.*
- **C3 — Do-no-harm, out-of-sample:** with a **held-out / conformal** threshold (not in-sample), the gate guarantees quality ≥ the feasible baseline **and rescues accuracy when that baseline is degraded** (small model / over-window / OOD), with a stated coverage. *Evidence: §gate-CV + a conformal variant.*

## 5. Intrinsic-characteristics study — ≥20 probes (cross-domain vs in-domain/in-task) → **filter**
> Setup for every probe: fix a trained GCM (e.g., Qwen3-8B). Conditions = **{in-task, cross-task, cross-domain}** eval sets (+ optionally `compress` vs `full` paths). Collect per-item tensors; compare distributions across conditions. Goal: find signatures that **distinguish** cross- vs in-domain AND ideally **predict** the compress/transfer drop (correlate probe ↔ per-item compress-correct). Each tagged with cost (🟢 cheap eval-only / 🟡 medium / 🔴 heavy).

### Feature / representation distribution
1. 🟢 **M-norm vs embedding-manifold ratio** — ‖M‖ / mean‖emb‖ by condition (does M drift off-manifold OOD?).
2. 🟢 **M ↔ full-ctx hidden CKA** (last & mid layer) per condition — representational fidelity of M vs full (CKA #124).
3. 🟢 **Effective/intrinsic dimension of M** (PCA participation ratio) in vs cross-domain (#125).
4. 🟢 **Hidden-state OOD score** (Mahalanobis/energy on layer-ℓ hidden of [M;q]) vs an in-domain reference (#109) — does it predict compress failure?
5. 🟡 **SAE feature overlap** between in- and cross-domain (fraction of shared active features; STS-style #104/#123) — predicts transfer?
6. 🟢 **Answer-token logit margin / entropy** (compress vs full) by condition — already our gate signals; test as transfer predictors.
7. 🟡 **Reconstruction loss of M** (`neg_recon`) by condition — does M preserve cross-domain ctx worse?
8. 🟢 **KL(Mq‖M0)** (query-shift) by condition — is query-conditioning more/less load-bearing OOD?
9. 🟡 **Centered kernel alignment of M across domains** — cluster structure of M by source domain (t-SNE/UMAP qualitative + silhouette).
10. 🟢 **Per-dim variance / "dead dims" of M** in vs cross-domain (capacity utilization).

### Attention / circuit signatures
11. 🟡 **Attention mass on M tokens** (reader: fraction of answer-position attention to M vs query) by condition — does the reader *use* M less OOD?
12. 🟡 **Retrieval-head engagement** (Wu 2025 #120): activation of known retrieval heads on M vs full, in vs cross-domain.
13. 🟡 **Induction-head usage** (#119) by condition — copy/identifier tasks (bfcl_multiple) vs semantic.
14. 🟢 **Attention entropy / dispersion by layer** (PyramidKV §3 #60) — in vs cross-domain sparsification curves.
15. 🟡 **Attention-sink mass** (#121) on M-prefix vs raw-ctx, by condition.
16. 🔴 **Activation patching**: patch M-induced residual into the full-ctx run; measure recovered accuracy by condition (causal info content of M, #126).
17. 🟡 **Logit-lens depth localization** (#122): at which layer does the answer become decodable from [M;q], in vs cross-domain?
18. 🟡 **Head-role taxonomy shift** (FastGen #61 local/special/broad): does the head mix the reader uses change OOD?

### Behavioral / predictive
19. 🟢 **compress−full gap distribution** per condition (headroom) — the quantity the gate must predict; its dispersion in vs cross-domain.
20. 🟢 **Calibration of gate signal** (reliability diagram / ECE) in vs cross-domain — does the same τ transfer? (motivates conformal.)
21. 🟢 **Exact-identifier vs semantic split**: stratify items by "answer is a rare verbatim string" (bfcl_multiple-like) vs paraphrasable; compress-gap per stratum × condition (explains the identifier-copy failure).
22. 🟡 **Length-stratified compress curve** per condition (does cross-domain hurt more at long ctx?).
23. 🟢 **Token-frequency / surprisal of gold** by condition (ties to "hidden stats drive transfer" #101).

**Selection rule:** keep the probes that (a) **separate** cross- vs in-domain with large effect size, AND (b) **correlate** with per-item compress-correct / transfer drop (so they *explain*, not just describe). Hypothesized survivors: **#2 (M↔full CKA), #4 (hidden OOD score), #11 (attention-mass-on-M), #19/#21 (headroom + identifier split), #20 (gate calibration/ECE)** — these map cleanly onto the three claims and the gate.

**Deliverable:** an analysis notebook/script that dumps per-item probe tensors for the {in,cross}-domain sets on a fixed adapter, computes effect sizes + probe↔correct correlations, and emits a ranked table → we down-select to 4–6 for the paper figure.
