# Paper B — consolidated report (2026-07-08 → 07-13)

> ⚠️ **`full` = TRUNCATED to MAXCTX (16k)**, not the whole document (`embed(ctx[:,:MAXCTX])`). It equals *true full* only when a doc ≤ 16k — true for every bench here **except ∞Bench** (131k docs → `full` sees ~12%). Only `rag` and our `auto`(chunk) read the whole doc; `full/window/tome/kvzip/knorm` are MAXCTX-bounded. See [`correctness-audit-2026-07-13.md`](correctness-audit-2026-07-13.md).

One report covering all experiments, every problem found & fixed, all analyses, the best result, and where we stand vs **all** baselines. Companion detail docs linked inline. Base = **Qwen3-8B** (frozen) unless noted; scores ×100; keep = 0.5.

---

## 0. TL;DR
- **Thesis (validated):** long-context accuracy is a *keep-the-answer* problem; a fixed compressor can't be right everywhere; selecting the right spans on a **frozen** base recovers or exceeds `full`. This diagnosis is **model-invariant across 9 families / 3 architectures / 1.7B–14B** (F43).
- **Our method (IMP):** a training-free, architecture-agnostic importance-router with modes {span, chunk, hier, bm25span, qfree, **auto**}. Best config: `auto` (options-aware `mc` router) + `signal=all` + **full-doc retrieval** for the chunk family.
- **Honest standing vs best-of-ALL-baselines:** IMP is **not** the most accurate method overall. It **wins outright on 3 benches**, **ties on ~5**, and **loses on literary-MC (to kvzip/knorm), multi-hop, MuSR**. Its defensible niche = **lossless needle retrieval + runs on cache-free linear + one router that's never far from the best arch-agnostic baseline**.
- **This week's headline fix:** the ∞Bench −19 "loss" was a **truncation artifact** (IMP saw the first 16k of 131k-token docs; RAG saw all). Fixed → **chunk full-doc 76.0 beats RAG (64–70) and full (57)** — the gap flipped to a win (F44).

**Correction the "all-baselines" view forces:** my earlier "IMP beats RAG on hard-MC" is misleading — on QuALITY-hard the real best is **kvzip/knorm ≈ 30**, and IMP loses by 17. IMP only "wins" hard-MC *within* the arch-agnostic set (and even there `no_ctx` 19.6 tops all methods).

**When IMP ties on accuracy, its extra advantages are thin & honest:** (i) **lossless on needles** (RULER 96.8 vs ToMe 0 / window 6); (ii) **runs on cache-free linear/GDN** where kvzip/knorm can't; (iii) **one router across regimes** (never collapses, always within ~0–3 of best arch-agnostic on QA). It is **not** cheaper or more accurate than RAG on lexical QA — RAG is a strong, free baseline.

## 5. Method (final config)
`auto` selector on frozen base: options-present → `span` (token-importance, needs forward, truncates to MAXCTX); else → `chunk` (256-tok BM25 over the **full untruncated doc**, no forward). `signal=all` (query-relevance + surprisal + IDF-lexical), span 32 / chunk 256, keep 0.5, `FULLDOC=1`. Evolution: token-IMP → span → +lexical → 5-mode redesign → auto(mc) → full-doc. Detail: [`imp-redesign-results.md`](imp-redesign-results.md), [`imp-method-and-implementation.md`](imp-method-and-implementation.md).

## 6. Honest paper framing
Not "IMP beats everything." The defensible contributions:
1. **The diagnosis** — a fixed compressor can't win everywhere; long context is often a liability; the chunk↔span crossover is input-predictable; RAG-style denoising can exceed `full` — **all model-invariant across 9 families (F27/F37/F39/F43).**
2. **IMP as the one arch-agnostic router** that never collapses (unlike ToMe/window), covers the **linear** regime KV-methods can't, and stays within ~0–3 of the best arch-agnostic baseline on QA while being lossless on needles.
3. **Rigor/honesty**: every number full-test or disclosed-N; a real bug (F44) found by case-level audit and fixed (flipping ∞Bench); negative results (F32/F38/F42) kept; best-of-**all**-baselines comparison showing where IMP loses (kvzip on literary-MC).
