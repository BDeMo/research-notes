## 1 · The do-no-harm gate ≈ adaptive retrieval / "when to use the augmentation"

This is the **closest conceptual family** to v1.5's gate: decide *per input* whether
to use the external augmentation, otherwise fall back to the base — to avoid the
augmentation *hurting* quality. v1.5 gates a soft-**memory wrapper**; this line gates
**text retrieval**, but the decision structure is identical.

| paper | id / venue | what | vs v1.5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TARG — Training-free Adaptive Retrieval Gating** | [2511.09803](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09803) · ICLR'26 sub | **single-shot gate from the model's own prefix logits** (mean entropy, **top-1/2 margin**, small-N variance); "retrieving for every query often hurts quality" → gate it; model-agnostic, no training | **almost the same gate idea**; gates *retrieval* w/ *logit* signals. v1.5 gates the *memory wrapper* w/ *memory-write-dynamics* signals (`delta_last`) — and finds those transfer cross-model better than logit/confidence signals (our H1). Closely cite + contrast. |
| **Self-RAG** | Asai et al. 2024 | trains reflection/control tokens to decide when to retrieve & critique | trained control tokens vs our training-free intrinsic-signal gate |
| **FLARE** | Jiang et al. 2023 | forward-looking active retrieval; re-query when predicted tokens look low-confidence | confidence-triggered, generation-time |
| **CRAG** | Yan et al. 2024 | scores retrieved-passage quality, corrective action when weak | quality of evidence, not "should I use it at all" |
| **SeaKR / SUGAR** | Yao et al. 2024 / 2025 | uncertainty probes modulate retrieval frequency | uncertainty-gated, like ours |
| **When Do LLMs Need Retrieval Augmentation?** | [2402.11457](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.11457) | confidence-gated retrieval; "retrieval may mislead → use only when uncertain" | the canonical "do-no-harm by gating" statement, for retrieval |
| **Adaptive Retrieval w/o Self-Knowledge? Uncertainty** | [2501.12835](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12835) | retrieve based on LLM self-knowledge / internal-state uncertainty | internal-state gating; our `delta_last` is a memory-side internal signal |

→ **v1.5's gate is the adaptive-retrieval idea applied to soft-prompt memory.** The
defensible novelty is *which* intrinsic signal gates it (memory-write dynamics, not
logits) and that it **transfers across 7 model families** — TARG itself notes logit
entropy "compresses as backbones sharpen" (echoes our logit-lens-is-model-specific
finding).

## 2 · Multi-depth injection (probe_deep) ≈ deep prefix / LLaMA-Adapter

The multi-layer-injection idea is **well-precedented**:

| paper | id / venue | what | vs probe_deep |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Prefix-Tuning** | Li & Liang 2021, [2101.00190](https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.00190) | trainable prefix vectors at **every** transformer layer (not just input) | the original "inject at all layers" |
| **P-Tuning v2** | Liu et al. 2021, [2110.07602](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.07602) | deep prompt tuning: learnable prompts at all layers | deep-prompt baseline |
| **LLaMA-Adapter** | Zhang et al., [2303.16199](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.16199) · ICLR'24 | inserts adaption prompts at the **topmost L layers** of a **frozen** LLM with a **per-layer zero-init gating factor** (gate starts 0 ⇒ preserve pretrained knowledge ⇒ progressively open); argues higher layers tune higher-level semantics | **almost exactly probe_deep's design** (per-layer gated injection, zero-init for do-no-harm-at-start). Our smoke's late-layer gate opening **reproduces their "topmost L layers" intuition.** |

→ **probe_deep is LLaMA-Adapter-style gated layer-wise injection, but for compressed
CONTEXT MEMORY** (LLaMA-Adapter injects instruction prompts). The contribution is the
*per-layer gate-activation map for memory* (which depth compressed memory helps), not
the mechanism. **Must cite LLaMA-Adapter + prefix-tuning prominently.**

## 3 · Known PROBLEMS of latent / soft-prompt memory (what v1.5 re-confirms)

The capacity/degradation failures v1.5 measures are **already documented**:

| problem | paper | finding |
|---|---|---|
| **lossy → exact-recall collapse** | **"A Silver Bullet or a Compromise?" (gist-token study)** [2412.17483](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.17483) · ACL'25 | gist/soft compression near-lossless on RAG/QA/summarization but **severe failure on synthetic recall & reranking**; 3 patterns: *lost by the boundary*, *lost if surprise*, *lost along the way*; bottleneck is in the gist reps. **= our RULER-NIAH→0 / "bit-capacity wall".** |
| **compressed memory DEGRADES the base (negative transfer)** | **"Rethinking Soft Compression in RAG" (SeleCom)** [2602.15856](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15856) | *proves* full soft-compression is (I) infeasible (incompatible w/ instruction-following/QA) & (II) unnecessary; compressed embeds **"attract disproportionate attention, blinding the LLM"** ⇒ degraded core function. Fix: **query-conditioned selector**. **= our negative transfer; their fix is a cousin of our query-conditioned gate.** |
| **limited capacity, drops entities/numbers** | **Information Preservation in Prompt Compression** EMNLP'25 findings | soft prompting can't encode cardinals/named-entities; proposes hybrid soft-hard (+23%) |
| **high compression loses too much** | **PCC — Pretraining Context Compressor** ACL'25 | 256× compression "loses too much useful info"; even compression across segments is suboptimal |
| **embedding-space capacity bounds** | Memory Tokens (≤240 tok/1 vec), Cramming 1568 tokens [2505.21189](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.21189) | how much one soft vector can hold — the capacity floor |

Context-compression ancestors (the wrapper itself): **Gist Tokens** (Mu 2023),
**AutoCompressor** (Chevalier 2023), **ICAE** (Ge 2024), **CCM / Compressed Context
Memory** ([2312.03414](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.03414), conditional LoRA + frozen base
+ online), **xRAG / PISCO** (soft RAG compression).

## 4 · Honest novelty assessment for v1.5

**Each piece has a close neighbour:**
- the **gate** ⇒ adaptive retrieval / TARG / Self-RAG (gate the augmentation per-input via intrinsic signals);
- **multi-depth injection** ⇒ LLaMA-Adapter / prefix-tuning / P-tuning-v2;
- the **capacity wall / exact-recall failure** ⇒ the gist "Silver Bullet" study, info-preservation, PCC;
- the **base-degradation** ⇒ "Rethinking Soft Compression" (compressed embeds blind the LLM); their query-conditioned selector ≈ our query-conditioned gate.

**The narrow, defensible white space (combination, not any single piece):**
1. **Gating a soft-prompt MEMORY wrapper** (not text retrieval) on a strictly frozen base — adaptive-retrieval's gate has not been ported to learned memory.
2. **The gating SIGNAL = memory-write dynamics** (`delta_last`, slot geometry), shown to **transfer across 7 model families** while logit/confidence signals do **not** (H1/H2). TARG/adaptive-retrieval use logit signals and even note they degrade across backbones.
3. The **capability-boundary characterization** (in-dist helps, sharp distribution-bound decay) for a memory wrapper.

**Honest risks (per meta-methodology §1):** do NOT pitch "do-no-harm gating", "multi-layer injection", or "soft-prompt capacity wall" as novel — each is anticipated. Pitch = *the cross-model-general memory-intrinsic gate signal* + the honest characterization. Head-to-head baselines a reviewer will demand: **TARG-style logit-margin gate**, **LLaMA-Adapter (deep gated injection)**, and a **query-conditioned selector (SeleCom)**.
